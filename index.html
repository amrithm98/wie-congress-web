About IEEE

IEEE is the worldâ€™slargest professional association dedicated to advancing technological innovation and excellence for the benefit of humanity. IEEE and its members inspire a global community through its highly cited publication, conferences, technology standards, and professional and educational activities. It has more than 426,000 members in more than 160 countries. IEEE was formed in 1963 after a merger between the Institute of Radio Engineers(IRE), founded in 1912, and American Institute of Electrical Engineers(AIEE). IEEE is now the leading authority on a range of technology including aero space system, computers, telecommunication, biomedical engineering, electric power,consumer electronics and many more. The IEEE serves more than to just provide a networking platform for professionals. It sponsors more than 1,600 conferences in 91 countries annually to aid the progression to technology. With 2,669 student branches at colleges and universities in 80 countries, it has a membership population of nearly 117,000 students.

About WIE

IEEE Women in Engineering (WIE) is the largest international professional organization dedicated to promoting women engineers and scientists. The mission of IEEE WIE is to facilitate the recruitment and retention of women in technical disciplines globally. IEEE WIE envisions a vibrant community of IEEE women and men collectively using their diverse talents to innovate for the benefit of humanity.

About IEEE Malabar Hub

The IEEE MALABAR HUB is one of the three HUBS under the IEEE KERALA SECTION. The Malabar HUB is spread throughout the Malabar Coast. The Malabar Coast banquets athwart the magnificent and ravishing situates of the northern part Kerala and so does the IEEE Malabar Hub. The IEEE Malabar HUB encompasses all the student branches from Palakad, Kannur, Kozhikode, Wayanad, Malappuram and Kasargod making it the biggest hub of the IEEE KERALA SECTION, in terms of area. The hub comprises of vibrant as well as enthusiastic volunteers across the hub and has always kept up their names and spirit in each of the Malabar hub events.
